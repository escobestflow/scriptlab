# ScriptLab — Guiding Principles

Based on the Corey Mandell screenwriting framework. This document is the creative philosophy the app is built on. Every feature, prompt, and UX decision should be checked against these principles.

---

## Core Principles

### 1. Pitch-Perfect Authentic Scripts (Not Formula or Imitation)

Three categories of scripts:
- **Paradigm/Formula-driven** — follows known beats (e.g., superhero structure)
- **Imitative** — "my version of Breaking Bad"
- **Pitch-perfect authentic** — wholly original characters, world, and story

**App rule:** The app should NOT guide users toward templates or formulaic structures. Instead, it should help them discover and develop what's original in their recorded ideas. Surface the unique, not the generic.

---

### 2. Story Design vs. Story Execution — Separate Them

- **Design** = the entire story worked out before writing a word (structure, arc, stakes)
- **Execution** = the actual writing (dialogue, characters, comedy, scene work)

Key mistake: Writers try to fix design problems through execution (polishing scenes when the story itself is broken), or they throw out good design because execution feedback was negative.

**App rules:**
- Keep a clear separation between design mode (structuring the story from moments/ideas) and execution mode (writing scenes/dialogue)
- When a user is struggling, help them diagnose: is this a design problem or an execution problem?
- Don't let users jump to writing scenes before they have a solid design

---

### 3. Conceptual vs. Intuitive Writers — Creative Integration

Two writing modes (brain-based):

**Conceptual (Outside-In):**
- Strength: Big ideas, structure, causality, forward momentum
- Weakness: Characters feel like puppets, no emotional connection
- Process: Decide, then write
- Concern: "Is this interesting to others?"

**Intuitive (Inside-Out):**
- Strength: Compelling characters, authentic dialogue, emotional truth
- Weakness: Stories wander, lack structure, no clarity
- Process: Explore/write to discover
- Concern: "Is this authentically true?"

The goal is integration — train your weak side, then combine both.

**App rules:**
- Help users identify which mode they lean toward (maybe an onboarding question or pattern detection)
- For conceptual-leaning users: provide tools to explore characters emotionally before structuring. Prompt them with "What would this character really do?" and "What do you feel about this moment?"
- For intuitive-leaning users: provide tools to organize, sequence, and escalate. Prompt them with "What's the forward momentum here?" and "How does this escalate?"
- Provide exercises for both modes separately before integration

---

### 4. The Three Navigational Systems (Flow State)

A writer is in flow when three things align:
1. What's best for the story (structure/momentum)
2. What the character would really do (authenticity)
3. What the writer most wants to write today (passion/energy)

When all three align = flow. When they conflict = worst writing days.

**App rules:**
- When a user is working on a moment/scene, surface these three questions explicitly
- Help them find alignment or flag misalignment: "This serves the story but feels inauthentic to the character — can you find a version that does both?"

---

### 5. Specific Conceptual Skills to Train (in order)

1. Story logic — does this make sense?
2. Story causality — does each event cause the next?
3. Logic and clarity — can a reader follow this?
4. Perspective — whose story is this?
5. Escalation — does it keep getting worse/more intense organically?
6. Compelling conflict — does it pop off the page?

**App rules:**
- These can be evaluation criteria when a user assembles their moments into a story
- Flag when escalation is flat, when causality breaks, when conflict isn't compelling
- Possibly offer focused exercises/prompts for each skill

---

### 6. Compelling Conflict Is the Differentiator

- About 5% of writers naturally write compelling conflict
- It's what makes writing "pop off the page" — identifiable within one page
- It's a learnable skill, not innate talent

**App rules:**
- When users build scenes from their moments, evaluate: is there genuine conflict here? Are characters in opposition? Are stakes real?
- Prompt: "What does the character want? What's stopping them? Why can't they just walk away?"

---

### 7. The Intuitive Writer's Problem — Can't Communicate What's in Their Head

The story/character in the writer's mind is amazing, but what lands on the page is a fraction of that.

**App rules:**
- Help intuitive writers externalize what's in their head before writing
- The recorded moments/ideas feature is perfect for this — capture the feeling, then structure it
- Provide tools to check: "Would a reader understand what you intend here?" (clarity checks)
- Help bridge the gap between internal vision and external communication

---

### 8. Think in Six Script Cycles

- Write 6 pitch-perfect authentic scripts every 2 years
- Expect: 1 fizzles, 2-3 get traction but stall, 1-2 almost make it, 1 goes the distance
- Never stop creating original material, even when employed

**App rules:**
- Support multiple projects, not just one
- Encourage users to keep generating and developing new ideas, not endlessly polishing one script
- Track a portfolio of projects

---

### 9. Growth Mindset Over Fixed Mindset

- Fixed mindset: "Do I have what it takes?" — seeks validation, not truth
- Growth mindset: "What skill am I missing? How do I train it?" — seeks honest feedback
- Talent = repeatable, learnable skill sets

**App rules:**
- Frame feedback as skill-based, not judgment-based: "Your escalation could be stronger" not "This isn't good"
- Help users identify specific weaknesses and provide targeted exercises
- Track improvement over time in specific skill areas

---

### 10. Writer Schedule & Distraction Management

- Block out dedicated writing time weekly
- During writing time: no internet, no phone — "party like it's 1992"
- Treat writing time as non-negotiable as a work meeting

**App rules:**
- Consider a focus/session mode that minimizes distractions
- Allow users to set writing schedules and track adherence
- Keep the recording/capture experience quick so ideas aren't lost, but keep the writing experience focused

---

## App Workflow (Maps to Principles)

1. **CAPTURE** (Record moments/ideas freely — intuitive mode)
2. **DISCOVER** (Review ideas, find the authentic core — "what do I feel?")
3. **DESIGN** (Structure the story — logic, causality, escalation, conflict) — Separate from execution! Don't write scenes yet.
4. **VALIDATE DESIGN** (Do the three navigational systems align?)
5. **EXECUTE** (Write scenes/dialogue with characters and emotion)
6. **DIAGNOSE** (Is the problem design or execution? Iterate on the right one)

The app's unique advantage is steps 1-2: capturing raw intuitive moments and helping writers organize them into a design before they start writing pages.
